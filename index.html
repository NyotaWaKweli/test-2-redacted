This style has been used where readers address a situation through their own perspective. The blanks are intentional. What you fill in depends entirely on you. Two people can fill the same blank with completely different words and both are right. There is no wrong version. Just different perspectives.

Below are four examples of how different moods can transform the same poem. Swipe sideways through them.

Four Moods, One Structure
Sad
I called you
and you left
the silence
we waited
in the dark
where nothing
used to hurt
Now I cry
the ghost
alone.

I chose you
and you held
the promise
we danced
in the garden
where roses
used to grow
Now I keep
the ring
alone.
Joy
I found you
and you became
the reason
we laughed
in the kitchen
where coffee
used to bloom
Now I drink
the memory
alone.
Heartbreak
I lost you
and you forgot
the photo
we ended
in the hospital
where machines
used to beep
Now I delete
the evidence
alone.

As you can see, the same poem. The same structure. Only a few words changed. But the entire meaning shifts. Same bones, different skin.

So there is no universal way to fill those blanks. It depends entirely on how you see the world from your side. Your life experiences. Your mindset. The things that shaped you.

This challenge doesn't seek perfection. It seeks expression. It's for those brave enough to write down their minds through alphabets.
